Abstract

Modern imitation-based robotic policies are predominantly expert systems: large pretrained Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models typically require test-time fine-tuning on task data to specialize their behavior. [cite: 42] We propose a new paradigm for robotic test-time adaptation: generating task-specific policy parameters directly in weight space to enable scalable zero-shot adaptation to unseen tasks. [cite: 43]

We introduce WIZARD, a meta-network that generates low-rank adapters for a frozen VLA policy from task evidence composed of language instructions and short demonstration videos. At inference time, given only a prompt and a single video from an unseen task, WIZARD generates the task-specific LoRA adapter in a single forward pass and applies it to the frozen VLA backbone. [cite: 47] Experiments on the LIBERO benchmark show that the generated adapters improve performance by up to ~ 2x on unseen datasets and up to ~ 15x on unseen tasks. [cite: 48]
