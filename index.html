WIZARD.
Robotic Policy Adaptation via Weight-Space Meta-Learning

WIZARD generates task-specific LoRA parameters directly in weight space to enable scalable zero-shot adaptation of VLA models. By passing a prompt and a single demonstration video through a meta-network, it outputs tailored policy weights in a single forward pass—bypassing the need for gradient-based test-time fine-tuning .

Methodology & Analysis.

The Weight-Space Formulation: We frame robotic adaptation as parameter inference. WIZARD directly maps visual and linguistic inputs to low-rank policy updates, using scale-aware generation and weight-space supervision to ensure stable control.

Key Ablations: Our analysis reveals that task-level embeddings preserve discriminative semantics necessary for effective parameter generation, outperforming dataset-level conditioning. Furthermore, a single episode is sufficient to construct an informative task embedding; expanding the support size yields diminishing returns. Finally, ablating the input modalities confirms that both text (providing the high-level intent) and visual demonstrations (providing geometric and kinematic priors) are critical to prevent performance collapse.

Zero-Shot Generalization: LIBERO-Spatial.

We evaluate weight-space inference against standard fine-tuning under strict held-out distribution shifts. In LIBERO-Spatial, WIZARD achieves a ~2x performance increase over the multi-task baseline entirely zero-shot.
